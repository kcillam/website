About Me

I am a second year PhD student in the  Department of Statistics and Data Science  at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. My broad research interests are in the fields of Causal Inference (CI) and Machine Learning (ML). I am interested in non-parametric identification and doubly robust estimation problems in CI. In ML, I am motivated by high-dimensional statistical and reinforcement learning problems, particularly when dealing with Large Language Models. I am fortunate to be jointly advised by Professor  Eric J. Tchetgen Tchetgen  and Professor   Edgar Dobriban.

Before starting my Ph.D., I did my Bachelors and Masters at the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. I completed my Master's Dissertation under Prof.   Bodhisattva Sen  at Columbia University, on the topic of Optimal Transportation. During my Masters, I also worked on shape-constrained regression problems as a Summer Research Intern at Carnegie Mellon University under the mentorship of Prof.  Arun Kuchibhotla.

Beyond statistics, I enjoy playing and following chess and badminton, and solving Rubik's cubes.
